El Pan De Muerto!


The Pan De Muerto is a baked good that we sell during the time of November in order to celebrate our loved ones who have since been deceased. "Pan de muerto brings to mind celebration, traditions, altars, culture, and how itâ€™s a part of our identity as Mexicans." Traditionally, pan de muerto is decorated with bone-like sticks and a skull-like ball at the top, all made from the same flour. There is, however, another element to consider if we want to fully understand the pan de muerto phenomenon, and that is the emotional connection that Mexicans have with it. This particular dish is present in most of the altars that are raised for the deceased family members during the Day of the Dead.

The Pan De Muerto is exclusively only sold during October-November as part of this tradition and will return again next year for the Day of The Dead!
